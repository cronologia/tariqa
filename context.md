## The subject

The **Tariqa Maryamiyya** is the Sufi order founded by **Frithjof Schuon**
(1907–1998) — a branch of the Algerian Alawiyya that became independent in
1936, Marian in devotion after Schuon's reported 1965 visions, and formally
"Maryamiyya" from 1969. It is the organizational heart of the **Traditionalist
School** (perennialism): the current of **René Guénon**, Schuon and **Ananda
Coomaraswamy** holding that all orthodox religions share one transcendent
truth. Headquarters moved from Lausanne (1941) to Bloomington, Indiana (1980);
after Schuon's retirement (1992) and death (1998) the order continued in
autonomous branches, commonly associated with Seyyed Hossein Nasr (Washington)
and Martin Lings (London).

## Why this project exists: the connections

- **To Catholic traditionalism (→ `cronologia/fsspx`):** Rama Coomaraswamy —
  perennialist author, sometime professor at the SSPX's US seminary, and
  sedevacantist priest — is the single most documented bridge between the two
  worlds (fsspx#19 holds the dossier).
- **To Brazil and the fsp critical literature (→ `cronologia/fsp`):**
  Olavo de Carvalho joined the order in 1986, served as Schuon's muqaddam in
  Brazil (the 1986/87 Brazilian branch — "the first tariqa brought to Brazil
  by Brazilians"), broke with Schuon at the end of the 1980s, and became the
  philosopher of the Brazilian right whose FSP criticism fsp already vaults.
- **To the glossary:** `tariqa`/Maryamiyya is a glossary entry this project
  deepens.

## The three "traditionalisms" (keep them apart)

1. **Traditionalist School** (this project): Guénon–Schuon perennialism.
2. **Catholic traditionalism** (fsspx): Lefebvre, Écône, sedevacantism —
   unrelated in origin; bridged only by individuals.
3. **Evola's political Traditionalism** and its Bannon/Dugin-era reception —
   a different adaptation of Guénon; Sedgwick's line on Olavo ("applied Guénon
   to political issues rather as Evola had, but unlike Evola combined
   Traditionalism with Roman Catholicism") shows how carefully these must be
   attributed.

## Standing sensitivities

- **Living people; a semi-private order.** Published scholarship (Sedgwick's
  *Against the Modern World*, his Traditionalists blog, the Aries article on
  Brazil) and participants' public writings only. No private members, ever.
- **The 1991 Bloomington affair** (indictment 15 Oct 1991 → all charges
  dismissed 20 Nov 1991, "not one shred of evidence" per the prosecutor as
  quoted by Wikipedia, never tried, never revived) is a single paired story:
  never mention one half without the other.
- **Internal dates are contested** (Schuon's initiation year, the 1936
  investiture accounts, the Brazilian founding 1986 vs 1987, Olavo's break):
  record the disagreement; do not resolve by preference.
- **Rupture narratives are partisan** (Schuon–Vâlsan 1950; Schuon–Olavo
  1980s): attribute every version, including the subject's own.
- **olavodecarvalho.org currently serves corrupted content** — cite Olavo's
  texts via Wayback snapshots only.
